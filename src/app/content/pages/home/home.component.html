About Us

Welcome to Nepal Cancer Hospital

Nepal Cancer Hospital and Research Center (NCHRC) Pvt. Ltd. is the first ever effort from the private sector, established to provide the highest quality state-of-the-art treatment and excellent care to the patients and their families from all around the globe. The hospital has a dedicated multidisciplinary team of cancer specialists offering affordable, safe, innovative and compassionate care to cancer patients. In addition to the latest innovative treatments and state-of-the-art equipment, the hospital goes beyond just medicine and opens access to alternative and complementary medicine as well.

With its multidisciplinary department that takes care of all types of cancer and cancer patients in the holistic approach under one roof, the hospital will contribute to reduce the burden of cancer, increase survival and improve the quality of life of the patients and families; thus, a special emphasis has been given to preventive and palliative oncology.

Extraordinary Services

Why You Should Choose Us?.

CURATIVE

Curative medicine is the health care given for medical conditions where a cure is considered achievable. We use a comprehensive modality consisting of Medical Oncology, Surgical Oncology and Radiation Oncology to treat cancerous diseases.

DIAGNOSTIC

Medical diagnosis is the process of determining which disease or condition explains a person’s symptoms and signs. We have the latest diagnostic facilities in our Laboratories and Imaging Departments to detect cancers in their earliest stages.

ACADEMIC & RESEARCH

NCH covers a very varied clinical spectrum and offers the highest quality and advanced oncology care

PALLIATIVE

Nepal Cancer Hospital and Research Center uses Comprehensive Cancer Control Programs for patients with terminal cancers focusing on their physical, social and emotional well being.

Message from the

Medical Director.

Though most of the cancers are curable if diagnosed early, people coming to hospitals for diagnosis and treatment in early stages are very low in Nepal. Even if they did, Nepal lacked the equipment and manpower that helped in proper and timely diagnosis. Due to lack of comprehensive cancer center patients were forced to go to abroad seeking the diagnosis and treatment of their disease. Besides that, illiteracy and ignorance of people played a significant role in increased cancer mortality among Nepalese people.
